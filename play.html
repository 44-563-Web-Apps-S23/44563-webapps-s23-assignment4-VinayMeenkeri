Vinay Meenkeri snips a play

MODESTY

by

Paul Hervieu_

JACQUES
. Good-by, Albert.

[_Exits after shaking hands cordially with_ ALBERT.

HENRIETTE
. [_Re-entering as_ ALBERT _assumes a rather severe attitude_.]

How are you? [_Pause._] Have you seen Jacques?

ALBERT
. [_With a determined air._] No, Henriette. Thank God!

HENRIETTE
. Why?

ALBERT
. Because it pains me to see men in your presence whom you care nothing for.

[_Delighted._] You don't like that?

[_Sitting down on sofa._

ALBERT
. No, I don't. And I'd like to tell you----

HENRIETTE
. About my relations with Jacques?
